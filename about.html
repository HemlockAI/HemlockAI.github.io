About Me

I am an explorer at the intersection of Philosophy, Psychology, and Artificial Intelligence. My mission is to understand the deeper implications of Artificial Intelligence and advocate for the ethical integration of AI to enhance human livelihood, ensuring that our humanity remains at the forefront of technological advancements.

I hold a B.S. in Psychology, with a Minor in Humanities and Philosophy, from Utah Tech University, where I delved into the crossroads between the fields of Philosophy, Psychology, and AI, addressing foundational questions and ethical frameworks necessary to navigate the complexities of AI and humanity. I am currently preparing to pursue a PhD, to further my research and expertise in these interdisciplinary fields.

My research focuses on the interdisciplinary dimensions of AI, its ethical and psychological impacts, and ensuring its integration supports human growth, creativity, and connection. I explore whether AI can be epistemologically compared to the human mind and align with our understanding of human nature, according to various philosophical and psychological insights from those before me whose shoulders I stand upon.

When I'm not immersed in exploring the ethical dimensions of AI, you can find me diving into a good book, getting lost in the latest blockbuster movie, or embarking on epic adventures in video games. I have a passion for learning new things, whether it's a new skill or a fascinating piece of trivia. Traveling allows me to experience different perspectives, while camping and fishing help me reconnect with nature.
